About PerAgri

Agriculture is undergoing a profound digital transformation driven by advances in pervasive sensing, artificial intelligence, edge computing, robotics, wireless communications, and cyber-physical systems. Modern farms increasingly deploy heterogeneous sensing infrastructures consisting of IoT devices, UAVs, satellites, autonomous ground vehicles, wearable devices, weather stations, and smart agricultural machinery. However, transforming these fragmented technologies into intelligent, scalable, and resilient agricultural ecosystems remains a significant research challenge.

PerAgri aims to provide a premier forum for researchers and practitioners working at the intersection of pervasive computing and precision agriculture. The workshop particularly encourages research that combines sensing, communication, computing, and intelligence into end-to-end pervasive agricultural systems rather than isolated technology components.

Call for Papers

The workshop will address fundamental and applied research challenges including:

Technical Issues Addressed

  • Pervasive sensing architectures for large-scale agricultural monitoring
  • Multi-modal sensing using ground sensors, UAVs, satellites, and mobile devices
  • Edge, fog, and cloud computing frameworks for agricultural intelligence
  • AI, TinyML, foundation models, and generative AI for agricultural decision support
  • Digital twins for farms, greenhouses, irrigation networks, and agricultural supply chains
  • Federated and distributed learning in resource-constrained agricultural environments
  • Autonomous UAVs, UGVs, and agricultural robotics

Target Community

PerAgri aims to attract a highly interdisciplinary audience spanning both pervasive computing and agricultural technology communities. The primary target participants include:
  • Pervasive Computing Researchers: Researchers working on IoT and sensor systems, edge computing, distributed AI, cyber-physical systems, and smart environments.
  • Agricultural Technology Researchers: Researchers focusing on precision agriculture, agricultural robotics, remote sensing, and digital agriculture.
  • Industry and Government Stakeholders: Agri-tech startups, precision farming solution providers, equipment manufacturers, and policymakers.
